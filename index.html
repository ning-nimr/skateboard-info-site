History of Skateboarding

1940s-1960s: The Birth

California surfers attached roller skate wheels to wooden boards, creating "sidewalk surfboards." First commercial boards from Makaha, Hobie, and Bing's featured clay wheels and basic trucks.

1970s: The Revolution

Frank Nasworthy introduced polyurethane wheels, dramatically improving performance. The Z-Boys from Dogtown pioneered pool riding, creating the foundation for vert skating culture.

1980s-1990s: Street Dominance

Alan "Ollie" Gelfand invented the ollie, revolutionizing skateboarding. Rodney Mullen adapted it for street skating, inventing the kickflip and establishing modern street skateboarding.

2000s-Present: Global Recognition

Skateboarding gained mainstream acceptance, culminating in its inclusion in the Olympics at Tokyo 2020. The sport continues evolving with new technology and global participation.
